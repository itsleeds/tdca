## Introduction

- This lecture bridges the gap between traditional data collection and
  modern data science.
- We move from static Excel-based analysis to dynamic, reproducible
  workflows.
- Connection to Semester 2: **Transport Data Science (TDS)**.
- Goal: Empower you with the tools of the future.

## What is Data Science?

> “A discipline that allows you to turn raw data into understanding,
> insight, and knowledge” (Wickham, Cetinkaya-Rundel, and Grolemund
> 2023)

- It is more than just statistics.
- It involves **programming**, **domain expertise**, and
  **communication**.
- In transport, it’s about handling “big data” from sensors, GPS, and
  smart cards (Boeing 2017).

## Why Reproducibility Matters

- **Reproducibility:** Others (or future you) can run your code and get
  the same results.
- **Excel vs. Code:**
  - Excel: Steps are often hidden in clicks; hard to audit.
  - Code: Every step is documented and repeatable.
- **Transparency:** Crucial for public policy and planning.

## The Power of Programming

- **Flexibility:** You are not limited by what a menu option allows.
- **Scalability:** The same code can process 10 rows or 10 million rows.
- **Sharing:** Open-source libraries mean you stand on the shoulders of
  giants.
- **Automation:** Spend more time thinking, less time copy-pasting.

## Tools of the Trade

- **Python:** A first-class citizen in data science. Versatile and
  powerful.
- **R:** Excellent for statistics and spatial analysis (Arribas-Bel
  2019).
- **Quarto:** The multi-language successor to R Markdown.
  - Mixes code, text, and output.
  - Renders to PDF, HTML, Word, and slides! (Allaire et al. 2024)

## Interactive Visualisations

- Made possible with code (e.g., Python’s `Streamlit`, `Plotly`, or R’s
  `Shiny`).
- **Impact:** Stakeholders can “play” with the data.
- Examples:
  - Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT)
  - Interactive road safety maps.
- *Increases the transparency and reach of research.*
